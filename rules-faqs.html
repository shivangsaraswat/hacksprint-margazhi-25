Official Rules

Overview

This document outlines the complete structure, rules, regulations, and selection criteria for HackSprint 2025. Please read it carefully to understand how the event will proceed and what is expected at each stage.

Eligibility

  • Only students of the IITM BS program are allowed to participate
  • All participants must register using their official IITM student email IDs

Team Formation

  • Participants can compete individually or in teams of up to 4 members
  • Teams must be finalized and communicated during the Team Formation session on Day 1.

Submission Rules

  • All projects must be submitted on GitHub with proper documentation, folder structure, and meaningful commit history.
  • Demo videos must be uploaded by the specified deadline.

Use of Tools

  • Participants are free to use any programming languages, frameworks, or tools.
  • Pre-built templates or codebases are not allowed without significant customization.

Ethics and Conduct

  • Plagiarism, unethical practices, or inappropriate behavior will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Communication on the Google Space community must remain professional and respectful.

Deadlines

  • Submissions must be made within the given timeframes. Late entries will not be accepted under any circumstances.

Event Flow Overview

HackSprint 2025 will feature two main rounds to evaluate and filter participants, culminating in the announcement of winners based on their submissions and demo presentations.

Day 1: Orientation and Idea Submission

  • Participants will attend an Orientation Session to understand event guidelines and themes.
  • Teams will brainstorm and submit their project ideas through a Google Form by 8:00 AM on Day 2.

Day 2: Shortlisting and Development

  • The Round 1 Results will be announced by 6:00 PM, shortlisting the top teams/individuals for the development phase.
  • Development begins at 9:00 PM.

Day 3: Development in Progress

  • Participants work on building their projects. Judges and mentors monitor progress through regular checkpoints.

Day 4: Final Submission

  • Projects must be submitted by 11:00 AM, including the GitHub repository and demo video.

Day 5: Results and Awards

  • Judges will evaluate all submissions, including the GitHub repository, documentation, and recorded demo video, to decide the winners.

Round-Wise Selection Criteria

The competition consists of a total of 2 rounds spread over 4 days.

Round 1: Idea Submission (Day 1 to Day 2)


Objective:
You will submit your project ideas to ensure they align with the provided themes, showcase originality, and are feasible within the hackathon timeframe.

Instructions:

  • Submit your project idea via the Google Form by 8:00 AM on Day 2.
  • Ensure your idea aligns with one of the provided themes, is creative and unique, and can realistically be developed during the hackathon.

Selection Criteria:
Your submissions will be reviewed based on the following points:

  • Theme Alignment: Does your idea match one of the provided themes?
  • Originality: How unique and innovative is your idea?
  • Feasibility: Can your idea be realistically implemented within the given timeframe?

Result Announcement: The shortlisted teams will be announced by 6:00 PM on Day 2


Round 2: Final Submission (Day 4)


Objective:
This round assesses your project’s development and execution based on your final submission.


What You Need to Submit:

1. GitHub Repository :
  • Ensure your repository is organized with a proper folder structure.
  • Include a detailed README file explaining your project, its features, and installation steps.
  • Maintain a meaningful commit history to reflect progress throughout the hackathon.
2. Demo Video :
  • Create a 2–3 minute video demonstrating your project’s functionality and explaining its purpose.

Judging Criteria

Your projects will be evaluated on these aspects:

  • Innovation & Creativity (30%): How unique and innovative is your solution?
  • Functionality & Execution (30%): Does the project function well and meet the intended purpose?
  • Relevance to Theme (20%): How closely does your project align with the chosen theme?
  • User Experience & Design (10%): Is your project user-friendly and well-designed?
  • Documentation & Presentation (10%): Is your README file detailed and your demo video clear?

Winners Announcement:
The winners will be announced during the Closing Ceremony on Day 5, based on the judges’ evaluations.


Important Notes for Participants


1. Be Prepared:

  • Ensure your GitHub repository and demo video are ready before submission deadlines
  • Familiarize yourself with the judging criteria to align your project accordingly.

2. Team Coordination:

  • Use Google Space effectively for team communication and event updates.
  • Stay in touch with event coordinators for support.

3. Focus on Originality:

  • Bring your unique ideas to life. Plagiarism will lead to immediate disqualification.

4. Time Management:

  • Use the time wisely to brainstorm, build, and refine your projects.
  • Follow the schedule and deadlines strictly.

Code of Conduct

To ensure a fair, engaging, and enjoyable experience for all participants, we have outlined the following code of conduct for HackSprint 2025. Participants are expected to adhere to these guidelines throughout the event. Failure to comply may result in disqualification. To that end we are asking everyone to abide by the principles outlined in HackSprint’s Code of Conduct.

FAQs

What is HackSprint 2025?

HackSprint 2025 is a 5-day online hackathon exclusively for students of the IITM BS program. It provides participants a platform to innovate, build, and showcase their web development skills through projects based on unique themes.

Who can participate in HackSprint 2025?

Only students enrolled in the IITM BS program are eligible to participate. Participants must register using their official IITM student email IDs.

Do I need a team to participate?

No, you can participate individually or in a team of up to 4 members. Teams must be finalized during the Team Formation & Communication Setup session on Day 1.

What is the schedule for the event?

The event runs from 2nd January 2025 to 6th January 2025. Key activities include:

  • Day 1: Orientation, team formation, and theme kickoff.
  • Day 2: Idea submission and shortlisting.
  • Day 4: Final project submission.
  • Day 5: Results and closing ceremony.

A detailed schedule is provided in the event materials.

How do I submit my project?

All projects must be submitted on GitHub with:

  • A properly organized repository
  • A README file containing project details and installation steps.
  • A demo video (2–3 minutes) showcasing the project’s functionality.

A link to the GitHub repository and the demo video must be submitted via the provided Google Form by the final submission deadline.

How will the projects be evaluated?

Projects will be judged based on the following criteria:

  • Innovation & Creativity (30%)
  • Functionality & Execution (30%)
  • Relevance to Theme (20%)
  • User Experience & Design (10%)
  • Documentation & Presentation (10%)

Judges will review the submissions, including GitHub repositories and demo videos, and assign scores based on these criteria.

What are the themes for the event?

The themes are broad and innovation-driven, designed to encourage creativity. You will receive the themes during the Problem Statement/Theme Kickoff session on Day 1.

Can I change my project idea after submission?

No, once your project idea is submitted during Round 1, it cannot be changed. Make sure your submission aligns with your chosen theme and is well-thought-out.

What happens if I miss a deadline?

Unfortunately, missing a deadline will result in disqualification, as the schedule is strictly enforced to ensure fairness and consistency.

Are there any restrictions on the tools or technologies I can use?

No, you can use any programming languages, frameworks, or tools to build your project. However, your project must not rely entirely on pre-built templates or AI-generated solutions. Significant customization is required.

How will I communicate with my team and the event coordinators?

All communication will be conducted via WhatsApp community groups. Make sure you join the designated group and stay active for updates, announcements, and support.

Are there any prizes for the winners?

Yes! The total prize pool is ₹12,250, divided among the top 10 winners:

  • 1st Place : ₹4,000
  • 2nd Place : ₹3,000
  • 3rd Place : ₹2,000
  • 4rd Place : ₹1,000
  • 5rd Place : ₹1,000
  • 6th Place : ₹250 (Amazon gift voucher)
  • 7th Place : ₹250 (Amazon gift voucher)
  • 8th Place : ₹250 (Amazon gift voucher)
  • 9th Place : ₹250 (Amazon gift voucher)
  • 10th Place : ₹250 (Amazon gift voucher)

Additionally, participation and winning certificates will be awarded to eligible participants.

What should I include in the demo video?

The demo video should be 2–3 minutes long and must include:

  • A brief explanation of your project idea.
  • A demonstration of the key features and functionality.
Will there be any speaker sessions?

Yes, we have planned speaker sessions to guide participants on technology, innovation, and best practices. These sessions will take place on Day 3 and Day 4.

Can I participate if I have no prior experience in hackathons?

Absolutely! HackSprint 2025 is beginner-friendly. Whether you’re an experienced developer or new to hackathons, the event is designed to help you learn and grow.

How will I know if my team has been shortlisted?

The results of Round 1: Idea Submission will be announced by 6:00 PM on Day 2. You will receive a notification in the WhatsApp community group.

What should I do if I encounter technical issues during the event?

If you face any issues related to GitHub, submission forms, or any other technical matters, raise your queries in the designated in Google Space.

Is there a fee to participate?

No, HackSprint 2025 is completely free for all IITM BS-DS students.

How will the winners be announced?

The winners will be announced during the Closing Ceremony on Day 5 after the judges complete their evaluations.

Who do I contact for further questions?

For any additional queries, contact the event coordinators through the Google Space group or email us at info@hacksprint.tech
